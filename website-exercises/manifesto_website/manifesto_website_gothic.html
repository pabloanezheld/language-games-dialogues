• INCANTATIONS •

Words of power spoken in ancient tongues, preserved from medieval grimoires and sacred texts.

  1. Vade Retro Satana — Greater Key of Solomon, 14th century
    "Vade retro Satana; nunquam suade mihi vana" • To command demons to depart.
  2. Ya Qahhar, Ya Qadir — Book of Healing, Avicenna, 11th century
    "Ya qahhar, ya qadir, ya latif" • Names of divine power invoked against ailment and curse.
  3. Abracadabra — Celsus, 2nd century Roman physician
    A diminishing charm, written in descending form to banish fever and sickness.
  4. Alaleu Invocation — Etruscan tomb inscriptions
    "Alaleu, alaleu" • Repeated cry for protection of the deceased in the underworld.
  5. Thrice-Holy Hymn — Byzantine liturgical texts
    "Hagios, Hagios, Hagios" • Sacred invocation repeated thrice to purify and sanctify.
  6. The Word of Unbinding — Goetia tradition
    "Agla, Tetagrammaton, Emmanuel" • Names used to dissolve bonds and restraints.
  7. Heka-Formulae — Egyptian Book of the Dead, 2nd millennium BCE
    "Sekhmet, Sekhmet, ward this place" • Invocation of the lioness goddess for protection.
  8. The Binding of Oaths — Norse skalds and völvas
    "By Odin, by Thor, by the threads of Wyrd" • Words spoken to seal pacts and compacts.
  9. Carmina Magica — Medieval Latin grimoires
    "Spiritus, anima, corporeum, luminale" • Called upon to bind spirit to flesh and will to word.
  10. The Shema Incantation — Kabbalistic texts, medieval period
    "Shema Yisrael, Adonai Eloheinu" • The most sacred utterance, invoked for protection and divine grace.

• POTIONS & DRAUGHTS •

Recipes drawn from herbals, alchemical treatises, and the wisdom of ancient apothecaries.

  1. Love Philtre — English manuscript, 15th century
    Rose petals, honey from a virgin hive, a strand of the beloved's hair, steeped in wine beneath the full moon for thirteen nights.
  2. Protection Draught — Papyrus Harris, ancient Egypt
    Mandrake root, serpent marrow, river barley, and beer—consumed at dawn to ward off malevolent spirits.
  3. Sleep Elixir — Dioscorides' De materia medica
    Juice of poppy, chamomile blooms, white wine, and crushed leaves of henbane, mixed before nightfall.
  4. Vitriol of Wisdom — Paracelsus' alchemical writings, 16th century
    Distilled spirits, mercury sublimate, cinnabar dust, and the ashes of scorpion—a single drop confers hidden knowledge.
  5. Panacea of Galen — Galenic medicine, 2nd century
    Sixteen rare spices, the marrow of a bird, serpent skin, opium, and frankincense boiled together under specific planetary hours.
  6. Invisibility Drench — Picatrix, 10th century Arabic magic
    Oil of thistle, fig juice, powdered wolf bone, sooted in darkness and applied to the brow.
  7. Draught of Truth-Speaking — Norse völva traditions
    Berries of the rowan tree, mead fermented with runes carved in oak, drunk to loose the tongue of lies.
  8. Embalming Unguent of Revival — Book of the Dead rituals
    Natron, myrrh, linen soaked in sacred oils, destined to preserve the body for the afterlife's journey.
  9. Aqua Fortis of Transmutation — Jabir ibn Hayyan (Geber), 8th century
    Nitric acid distilled from saltpeter, combined with aqua regia—a corrosive essence said to dissolve the base and elevate the precious.
  10. Celestial Tincture — Albrecht of Saxony, Renaissance alchemy
    Starflower mashed beneath the constellations, fermented in silver vials under the light of Jupiter—grants visions of distant lands.

• DIVINATIONS & PROPHECIES •

Methods by which the ancients glimpsed the future, preserved across cultures and centuries.

  1. Auspicy — Reading the Flight of Birds — Roman augural practice, Pliny the Elder
    The direction, species, and behavior of birds in the sky were interpreted as signs from Jupiter; practiced by the College of Augurs.
  2. Sortes Virgilianae — Medieval divination, practiced from 4th–17th centuries
    Random selection of lines from Virgil's works to predict the future; widely used by nobility and clergy across Christendom.
  3. The Oracle of Delphi — Ancient Greece, Herodotus
    The Pythia priestess inhaled vaporous vapors from a chasm and uttered cryptic pronouncements, interpreted by priests as divine will.
  4. Haruspicy — Entrail Reading — Etruscan and Roman practice
    Examination of the liver, heart, and intestines of sacrificed animals to divine the favor or displeasure of the gods.
  5. Geomancy — Arabic divinatory art, Al-Kindi, 9th century
    Sixteen figures formed by random dots cast upon sand or paper, interpreted according to complex astrological and numerical systems.
  6. Tarot Cartomancy — Italian practice, spread across Europe from 15th century onward
    A deck of seventy-eight painted cards arranged in patterns; each position and image reveals truths hidden from ordinary sight.
  7. Astromancy — Ptolemy's Tetrabiblos, 2nd century
    The positions of planets and stars at the moment of birth determine character, fate, and the currents of one's life.
  8. Scrying — Mirror Gazing — Medieval grimoire tradition
    A polished mirror or still water gazed upon in darkness reveals visions of distant events and hidden knowledge.
  9. Onomancy — Divination by Names — Kabbalistic tradition
    The hidden numbers within the letters of a name, when calculated according to Gematria, unveil the person's true nature and destiny.
  10. Cleromancy — The Cast of Lots — Ancient Near East and Biblical texts
    Stones, bones, or runes cast to the ground; their positions and arrangements reveal divine judgment and the path forward.
